# International Student Admissions Analytics – DePaul University

## Overview
This project analyzes international student application data for DePaul University to uncover
application trends, admission outcomes, and outreach effectiveness.

The project was completed during the Excelerate Data Visualization Internship and follows the
complete analytics lifecycle from raw data exploration to dashboard presentation.

---

## Dataset Summary
- Raw records: 7,543
- Cleaned records: ~5,000 unique applicants
- Attributes: 80 columns
- Data type: CRM-style admissions and outreach data

---

## Business Objectives
- Identify top applicant source countries
- Analyze program and intake demand
- Measure admission conversion rates
- Identify application pipeline bottlenecks
- Evaluate outreach and counselor performance

---

## Tools & Technologies
- Microsoft Excel (data cleaning & preparation)
- Power BI (dashboard development)
- Power Query (data transformation)
- DAX (KPIs and measures)

---

## Project Structure
- **Week 1:** Dataset exploration & data quality assessment  
- **Week 2:** Data cleaning, standardization & transformation  
- **Week 3:** Dashboard development & insight analysis  
- **Week 4:** Final presentation & stakeholder reporting  

---

## Dashboards Included
1. Applicant Trends by Country  
2. Program & Intake Distribution  
3. Admission Outcomes Analysis  
4. Outreach & Counselor Performance  

---

## Key Insights
- Over 50% of applicants originate from India.
- Fall intakes generate the highest application volume.
- Overall admission conversion rate: 65%.
- Missing transcripts are the largest admission blocker.
- Study Group applicants show higher conversion efficiency.
- Counselor outreach workload is unevenly distributed.

---

## Recommendations
- Diversify recruitment markets beyond India.
- Increase admissions staffing during peak Fall intake.
- Improve early-stage applicant engagement.
- Automate document tracking and reminders.
- Expand high-performing recruitment channels.
